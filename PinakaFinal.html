YHA GOALS

The Youth Health Ambassador program was established through the Tobacco-Free Generation movement – a social initiative aimed at reshaping the landscape of teenage smoking and vaping addiction. This movement began after the concept was published and recognized as part of the international Tobacco Control Endgame in 2011. The following year, in 2012, an international education ecosystem was launched to support this vision. Since then, thousands of youths from various countries have joined the cause, aspiring to become a well-informed, Tobacco-Free Generation that refuses to be the "replacement smokers" targeted by the tobacco industry.

ACCOMPLISHMENT

YHA Dinalupihan has accomplished several initiatives such as conducting awareness campaigns in schools, organizing youth seminars about the effects of smoking and vaping, and promoting healthy living through outreach programs. Members serve as role models and peer educators, influencing others to make better choices for their health and future.

Courtesy Call

On September 23, the newly elected Youth Health Ambassadors of Dinalupihan paid a courtesy visit to the local government office, accompanied by SB Member Noli Soriano, MFSK President Aries Atencio, and HEPO Tina Matawaran. These young ambassadors are set to partner with the local government in various projects and programs, particularly those focused on mental health awareness and promoting a Tobacco-Free Generation.

Toward a Tobacco Free Future

The Dinalupihan Youth Health Ambassadors—Miyore Kyle Morales, Khmer Zheldon Aquino, and Marvin Maniego—together with MHO Coordinator HEPO Tina Matawaran, attended the House of Representatives yesterday, November 19, as part of the celebration of Childrens Month. This was upon the invitation of our esteemed 3rd District Representative, Hon. Maria Angela Garcia, who also serves as the Vice Chairperson of the Committee on the Welfare of Children. They were also joined by Sangguniang Kabataan representatives Ms. Jasmine Rodriguez, Ms. Gillian Magcalas, and Mr. Michael James Dumapias. The program included a policy forum focused on protecting children and youth from the dangers of cigarettes and e-cigarettes, aiming to strengthen policies that safeguard their health and well-being.

YHA in Hataw Takbo Bataan

The Youth Health Ambassadors (YHA) proudly participated and volunteered in the Hataw Takbo Bataan – Dinalupihan Leg on June 21, 2025. The event was fun, meaningful, and full of energy, bringing the community together for a great cause.

YHA Chapters

In 2024, the Youth Health Ambassador (YHA) Dinalupihan proudly joined the growing YHA family alongside the newly established chapters of YHA Abucay and YHA Orani, adding to the existing YHA Balanga and YHA Mariveles. From the provincial to the local level, these chapters continued to expand the reach and strengthen the impact of the YHA advocacy. As 2024 comes to a close, the organization carries the hope and determination for 2025—a year filled with opportunities, broader outreach, and greater unity. Guided by the mantra "Pinalalawak ang Saklaw, Pinatatatag ang Adbokasiya," YHA aims to establish chapters in the remaining seven municipalities, reaching more youth and communities, and further promoting a healthier, tobacco-free generation.
